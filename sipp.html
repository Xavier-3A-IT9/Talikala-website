Effect of Household Derived Composts on the Height and Yield of Solanum lycopersicum L. (Tomato Plant)

SIP Title

Background/Significance of Study

Composting is valuable to the communities and the environment as it provides improved plant growth and harvest yield. Compost is defined as the product resulting from the controlled biological decomposition of organic material. In terms of benefiting from the freeway practice of composting, Talikala Inc. receives the convenience of healthier plant growth and harvest, particularly of tomato plants, within the community. In addition, composting in itself can assist this institution as it actually promotes positive effects on one’s holistic health. Harnessing the outdoor spaces of our homes with our composting of Solanum Lycopersicum L. (tomato plant) seeds, the added greenery can actively affect the emotional and psychological natures of our partner institution’s benefactors. This can be a great boost when used at Talikala’s facilities, providing much-needed greenery for improved comfort. In the long run, Talikala, Inc. can also be assisted through composting in reducing waste; food scraps and other organic materials are diverted from disposal and composted. Organic materials are a community asset and are generated and recycled into compost within the same local community which can be a good source of income.Through the use of compost in our SIP, we can share our results with the institution to teach them how to produce a higher yield of tomato fruits that can be sold to the market. Composting engages and educates the community in resource stewardship or community sustainability while providing solutions that empower individuals, businesses, and institutions to capture organic waste and retain it as a community resource.

Problem Statement

What is the effect of organic fertilizer (household derived composts) when used through drenching application compared to inorganic fertilizer (NPK) to the height and yield of Solanum lycopersicum L. (tomato plant)

Hypothesis

If an organic fertilizer, in this case, household derived composts is used through drenching application on the tomato plant, then the Solanum lycopersicum L. (tomato plant) will grow taller and will have higher yield in comparison to the plant using inorganic fertilizer (NPK) within a specific period of time.

Independent Variable
The independent variable is the type of fertilizer used on Solanum Lycopersicum L. (tomato plants). The type of fertilizers includes organic fertilizers and inorganic fertilizers. ● To test these types, we will be using household-derived composts as the organic fertilizer and NPK as the inorganic fertilizer. The raw materials for the household composts will be mixed ”browns” like dried leaves, used egg trays, cartons, or cardboard, and crushed eggshells; mixed “greens” such as Moringa (Malunggay) leaves, grass clippings, v egetable scraps, and banana peels. A bag of garden soil and water will also be needed in making organic compost. The composition of NPK fertilizer will be 14 Nitrogen, 14 Phosphorus, and 14 Potassium as the inorganic fertilizer.
Dependent Variable/s
The dependent variable would be the height of the tomato plant and number of yield of tomato fruit.
Controlled Setup
The controlled set-up will not receive any treatment for the whole experimentation.All replicates will be watered with 300 mL of tap water twice a day, will get full sunlight exposure for 5 hours daily, will use the same type of soil (garden soil), will use the same dosage/amount of soil ( 5 kg each replicate ), will have the same time/duration of planting (8 weeks), will use the same type of container (10 Liter water bottle), and will use the same variety of tomato seeds (Ramgo seeds.)
